### Early Septic Shock Clinical Intervention Guideline
**1. Trigger Conditions (Vital Sign Thresholds)**:
- Suspected or confirmed infection with systolic blood pressure <90 mmHg, mean arterial pressure <65 mmHg, or a clear drop from baseline.
- Elevated serum lactate, especially >=2 mmol/L with signs of hypoperfusion; if vasopressors are required to maintain MAP >=65 mmHg, treat as highly concerning for septic shock.
- Common accompanying findings include heart rate >100 beats/min, respiratory rate >22 breaths/min, reduced urine output, altered mental status, cool clammy skin, or prolonged capillary refill.

**2. Immediate Interventions**:
- Treat as a medical emergency; obtain blood cultures and relevant specimens immediately, but do not delay antibiotics, and aim to start broad-spectrum antimicrobials within 1 hour of recognition.
- Establish at least two IV lines and begin balanced crystalloid resuscitation promptly; in patients with hypotension or hypoperfusion, an initial target of 30 mL/kg within the first 3 hours is reasonable with dynamic reassessment.
- Measure and trend lactate, monitor urine output, perfusion, and blood pressure continuously, and urgently identify the infectious source for source control.
- If MAP remains <65 mmHg after fluid resuscitation, start norepinephrine promptly; add vasopressin when needed and avoid delays in vasopressor initiation.

**3. Contraindications**:
- Do not delay antimicrobial therapy while waiting for imaging, consultant evaluation, or culture results.
- Do not use hydroxyethyl starch for resuscitation; gelatin-based fluids are also not preferred for routine first-line use.
- In patients with severe heart failure, overt pulmonary edema, or anuric renal failure, fluid resuscitation must be more tightly guided with dynamic assessment to avoid indiscriminate fluid loading.
- Dopamine should not be used as the preferred first-line vasopressor, especially in patients at high risk for arrhythmia.

**4. Escalation**:
- Notify the on-call ICU resident and ICU attending immediately.
- If hypotension persists, lactate rises, vasopressors are needed, or intubation/CRRT is likely, escalate simultaneously to the ICU attending, infectious diseases, and the highest available critical care support level.
