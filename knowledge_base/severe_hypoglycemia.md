### Severe Hypoglycemia Clinical Intervention Guideline
**1. Trigger Conditions (Vital Sign Thresholds)**:
- Capillary or venous blood glucose <54 mg/dL (<3.0 mmol/L), or any hypoglycemic event requiring assistance from another person.
- Associated altered mental status, seizure, agitation, coma, recurrent diaphoresis, or tachycardia, often >100 beats/min.
- In ICU patients, any acute neurologic deterioration with suspected hypoglycemia and inability to take oral carbohydrates should be treated as severe hypoglycemia first.

**2. Immediate Interventions**:
- Immediately recheck bedside glucose and secure the airway; if mental status is impaired, stop oral intake and initiate continuous ECG, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation monitoring.
- If IV access is available, give IV dextrose promptly: 25 g glucose, such as 50 mL D50W, or an equivalent concentrated dextrose dose, then continue with D10 infusion based on repeat glucose testing.
- If IV access is not available, administer glucagon 1 mg IM or SC, or intranasal glucagon, and obtain IV access as soon as possible.
- Recheck glucose within 15 minutes; if glucose remains <70 mg/dL or mental status does not improve, repeat treatment and investigate the cause, including excess insulin, renal failure, sepsis, or interrupted nutrition.

**3. Contraindications**:
- Do not give oral carbohydrates to patients with impaired consciousness, dysphagia, or vomiting because of aspiration risk.
- Avoid glucagon in patients with pheochromocytoma; in suspected insulinoma, monitor closely for rebound hypoglycemia after glucagon.
- Extravasation of hypertonic dextrose can cause tissue injury; confirm IV patency and monitor the infusion site closely.
- After correction, reassess the glucose-lowering regimen; episodes related to long-acting insulin or sulfonylureas require prolonged monitoring because recurrence is common.

**4. Escalation**:
- Notify the on-call ICU resident or covering physician immediately.
- Escalate to the ICU attending and consider endocrinology consultation if seizures, coma, recurrent hypoglycemia, or prolonged high-concentration dextrose support is required.
