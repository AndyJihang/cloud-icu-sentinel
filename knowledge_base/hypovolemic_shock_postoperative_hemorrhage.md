### Postoperative Major Hemorrhage / Hypovolemic Shock Clinical Intervention Guideline
**1. Trigger Conditions (Vital Sign Thresholds)**:
- Sudden increase in postoperative drain output or wound bleeding accompanied by systolic blood pressure <90 mmHg, mean arterial pressure <65 mmHg, or a significant drop from baseline.
- Heart rate >110 beats/min, narrowed pulse pressure, cool clammy skin, urine output <0.5 mL/kg/h, or altered mental status.
- Rapid hemoglobin decline, rising lactate, or worsening base deficit indicating inadequate tissue perfusion.

**2. Immediate Interventions**:
- Activate the major hemorrhage or massive transfusion pathway immediately, obtain large-bore IV or central access, and continuously monitor vital signs while rapidly evaluating the bleeding source.
- Prioritize source control: notify the surgical team at once to assess for return to the operating room, bedside hemostatic intervention, or interventional radiology embolization.
- Use limited crystalloid resuscitation and transition quickly to blood product resuscitation; administer packed red blood cells, plasma, and platelets according to institutional protocol, while repeating coagulation studies, fibrinogen, and ionized calcium.
- Correct reversible factors including hypothermia, hypocalcemia, and acidosis; when appropriate under local protocol, consider early tranexamic acid.

**3. Contraindications**:
- Do not use large volumes of crystalloid as a substitute for blood product resuscitation in ongoing hemorrhage because this worsens dilutional coagulopathy and hypothermia.
- Do not delay activation of a massive transfusion pathway or surgical hemostasis while waiting for laboratory confirmation.
- In patients taking warfarin, direct oral anticoagulants, or antiplatelet agents, reversal strategy must be assessed urgently with appropriate specialist support.
- Blindly clamping drains, removing dressings that may be tamponading bleeding, or delaying repeat operative evaluation can worsen hemorrhage.

**4. Escalation**:
- Notify the on-call ICU resident, primary surgical team, and anesthesia or operating room coordination immediately.
- If hypotension persists, major transfusion is required, or return to the operating room / interventional hemostasis is likely, escalate immediately to the ICU attending and senior surgeon.
